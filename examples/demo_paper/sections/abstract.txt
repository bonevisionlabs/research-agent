This study presents an automated pipeline for image classification using deep convolutional neural networks. We evaluate three architectures on a benchmark dataset of 10,000 images across 10 categories, using 5-fold cross-validation to ensure robust performance estimates. Our proposed method achieves a mean accuracy of 92.3% (+/- 1.5%), significantly outperforming the baseline approach (85.2% +/- 3.0%, p < 0.01, Wilcoxon signed-rank test). Regional analysis reveals that classification accuracy varies across categories, with the highest performance on structured objects (96.1%) and lowest on natural scenes (87.4%). These results demonstrate that task-specific fine-tuning with appropriate data augmentation yields substantial improvements over general-purpose pre-trained models for domain-specific classification tasks.
